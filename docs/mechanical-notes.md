# Mechanical Notes

## Purpose
This document captures early mechanical ideas for the spindle, gripping head, and bottle interaction.

## Main Mechanical Concepts
- A primary hydraulic cylinder drives a spindle gear to create rotation
- The spindle applies rotational motion to the bottle top
- A three-cylinder gripping head helps guide and hold the bottle top
- A beveled circular housing helps self-center the top during application

## Key Mechanical Areas
- Spindle shaft and gear mechanism
- Head assembly for top engagement
- Three-cylinder grip arrangement
- Bottle support / fixturing
- Frame and guarding
- Interfaces between hydraulic motion and rotating head

## Design Considerations
- The top must be guided gently into place
- Grip force must be limited to avoid damaging the top or bottle
- Rotational torque must be controlled and monitored
- Mechanical alignment will be critical for repeatable operation
- Components in contact with the bottle and top may need replaceable tooling

## Open Questions
- What cap/top geometry is being handled?
- What spindle speed is required?
- How much vertical/downward travel is needed?
- Does the bottle remain stationary or require support/clamping?
- How will changeover be handled for different bottle sizes?
